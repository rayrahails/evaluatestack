The Problem

Most Companies Do Not Have an Evaluation System. They Have a Collection of Guesses.

As companies scale, the things they rely on to evaluate readiness quietly stop working.

Your Readiness Signals Are Unreliable

Someone passed. Someone was onboarded. Someone scored well. None of that tells you whether they can perform when the decision actually matters.

What Worked at 20 Collapses at 200

The evaluation process that got you here stops working as you scale. Standards drift. Signal degrades. Decisions that used to be obvious become arguments.

Decisions Get Made on Opinion

Every team defines "ready" differently. There is no shared standard. No shared measurement. The organization is making consequential decisions without a system to support them.

The real problem is not bad assessments. It is the absence of a system that defines what "ready" means, how it is measured, and how signal is preserved as the organization grows.

Perspective

This Was Not a People Problem. It Was a System Design Problem.

A cloud infrastructure platform team certified 40 developers as production-ready. Within the first quarter, 12 of them required significant support to handle real workloads. The developers were not the problem. The certification system was measuring the wrong things.

What the system tested

Knowledge recall. Framework vocabulary. Textbook scenarios with clear right answers.

What production actually required

Debugging under ambiguity. Making tradeoff decisions with incomplete data. Communicating failure modes to non-technical stakeholders. Navigating system dependencies they had never seen in a controlled environment.

The fix was not better questions. It was a redesigned system that measured capability at the level production actually demands. That is the work I do.
